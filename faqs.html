FAQs

Do I need to live near the milk bank or a depot to donate?

Donor screening takes place over the phone and electronically, so we are happy to talk with potential donors regardless of where they live. We can send boxes and prepaid shipping labels so you can easily ship your milk to us. Of course, if you would like to drop off milk at the milk bank or a depot, that’s always OK. We love to meet our donors!

How much do I need to commit to donating?

We request that each donor commit to donating 200 ounces. The cost of screening and testing is high, and our costs are lowered by approved donors providing more milk. You don’t have to send it all at once! You can if you want to, but it’s not required. We find that milk stays frozen best if you send at least 100 oz. per shipment.

Can I donate my previously collected milk?

We can take previously collected milk as long as it was collected less than 6 months ago and has been in the freezer the whole time. We would just need to go through the screening process with you to make sure you were following the medication restrictions and weren’t sick at any time while collecting the milk.

How long can I be a donor?

Once donor screening is finished, there is no limit on how long you can be a donor. We are happy to accept any milk that is pumped during the time that you are pumping for that baby.

Can you use my prenatal blood work instead of doing a new blood test?

Unfortunately, we cannot. The blood test is the last step in donor screening because we need to receive the first donation within 30 days of the test being done. Also, there are some tests we require that are rarely done during the prenatal period.

How does pasteurization affect the milk?

All donor milk is pasteurized to eliminate bacteria or other infecting organisms that may have been present. A small percentage of nutritional and immunological properties is destroyed by pasteurization, but pasteurized milk retains many of its most beneficial qualities. It contains many special properties that cannot be duplicated by commercial milk formulas.
